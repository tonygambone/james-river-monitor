About

This app tells you whether it's safe to be on the James River around Richmond, VA.

According to James River Park System rules, you need a life vest to be on the river if it is over 5 feet, and a high-water permit if it is over 9 feet. Measurements are taken at the Westham gauge, located just upstream of the Huguenot Bridge.

The source of the data is NOAA's hydrologic prediction service. This app takes into account the highest value, observed or predicted, for the current 24-hour period.

I made this app because I got tired of all the news stories about people getting themselves into trouble because of high water on the James. The rules are there for a reason; disregarding them risks not only your own safety, but that of the rescuers, and of anyone else in the city needing assistance when a rescue operation is underway. It also wastes a staggering amount of taxpayer dollars every time this happens.

Apparently the clearly posted signage and readily available information are too difficult for some folks to digest, so my aim is to take all the guesswork out of it.

I'm Tony Gambone, and I'm lucky enough to live near the park and to enjoy its many great qualities. Stay safe out there.
